Privacy Policy — Aquarius Road Department

Effective / last updated: 2026-07-19

The short version: we don't want your data. Aquarius Road Department (ARD) is built so that the thing most worth protecting — where you actually are or live on 2b2t/6b6t — is never collected in the first place, not filtered out after the fact. Beyond that, we collect as little as the project needs to function, we don't sell it, we don't share it with advertisers or data brokers, and we don't run analytics or tracking on this site.

What this project is

ARD is a crowdsourced map of nether-highway conditions (holes, lava, roadblocks, etc.) for anarchy Minecraft servers, fed by a proxy plugin / client mod and shown at map.aquariusconnect.org. The full technical protocol is public: PROTOCOL.md. This page is the plain-language privacy summary; if the two ever disagree, treat this page as authoritative for how your data is actually handled — the repo doc is the source of truth for how the system is built to enforce it.

The one guarantee that matters most

Your real in-game coordinates cannot be reconstructed from anything this project transmits or stores, even by us. The wire format has no `(x, z)` field at all — a contributor's client sends only an abstract, 1-D "how far along this known public road" value. The one piece of information that could actually locate you (how far off that road you were) is computed locally on your own device for a one-time sanity check and is discarded before any report is built — it is never sent, never logged, never stored, anywhere, by anyone.

What we collect, and why

  • Just browsing the map: nothing beyond ordinary web-server connection handling (see "Logging" below). No account, no login, no cookie, no tracking script.
  • Submitting a road-condition report (via the proxy plugin or client mod): the abstract on-road location above, the condition type, and — depending on how the report is authorized — either your IP address or a linked Discord identity, used transiently to compute a one-way corroboration signal (see "How we protect what we do collect"). Neither the raw IP nor the raw Discord ID is stored alongside the report itself.
  • Linking a Discord account (optional, only if you choose to for a lower corroboration threshold): we request only Discord's identify OAuth scope, and from Discord's response we extract and store only your numeric Discord user ID — nothing else Discord returns (username, avatar, discriminator, etc.) is read or stored. We never request the email scope and never see or store your email address, and we never see your Discord password (Discord's own login page handles that, not us). We also store the Minecraft UID you're linking it to, since that's the whole point of the link.
  • Admin/moderator dashboard login: the same Discord identify exchange as above, plus a session cookie — a random token whose hash (not the raw token) is what's actually stored, expires automatically within hours, and is only ever issued to a Discord identity someone has explicitly granted dashboard access to.

What we deliberately do not collect

  • Real, absolute in-game coordinates — architecturally impossible to submit, see above.
  • Your Discord email address, phone number, or any Discord scope beyond identify.
  • Any payment, billing, or financial information — this project doesn't charge for anything.
  • Analytics, ad-tracking, or third-party tracking scripts of any kind. The map itself is rendered with a copy of Leaflet hosted on our own server, not loaded from a third-party CDN — your browser doesn't make requests to anyone but this site just to view the map.
  • A persistent, cross-session tracking cookie. The only cookie this site ever sets is the short-lived admin/moderator session above, and only for people who deliberately sign in for that purpose.

How we protect what we do collect

  • Bearer tokens and session cookies: only a one-way SHA-256 hash is ever stored. The raw value is shown to you once, at issue time, and we cannot recover it afterward — if you lose it, we revoke and reissue rather than "look it up."
  • IP addresses and Discord IDs used for anonymous/Tier-B report corroboration: used only in memory, for the moment it takes to compute a salted SHA-256 hash scoped to that specific report location and condition type — the same input hashed for a different spot, or a different condition at the same spot, produces an unrelated, uncorrelatable hash. The salt is a random value generated fresh at process start and is not persisted, so it changes on every service restart. The raw IP/Discord ID is never written to disk.
  • Report data expires. Conditions and their corroboration records are automatically dropped after a few hours of no fresh confirmation (a "decay window") — we're not building a permanent historical record of anyone's activity, just a live conditions feed.
  • Revocation is real and immediate. A moderator or admin grant, a bearer token, or a linked account can be revoked or suspended, and it stops working on the very next request — there's no cache or delay to worry about.

Logging

The server process does not write a per-request access log at all — no method, path, query string, or client IP is written to disk or process output for ordinary map/report/API traffic. Coordinates are never logged anywhere in this codebase, full stop, regardless of report tier.

This site is reached through Cloudflare (for TLS and a private tunnel to the box it runs on, so nothing is directly exposed to the internet). Cloudflare, as our connection provider, sees standard connection metadata for requests to this domain the same way it would for any site using it — that's between you, Cloudflare, and their own privacy policy, not something we query, store, or use for tracking.

Third parties

Two, and only for the features that need them:

  • Discord — handles the actual login/consent step if you choose to link an account or sign into the dashboard. See Discord's privacy policy for what they collect on their side; we only ever receive what's described above.
  • Cloudflare — TLS and network transport for this domain, as described above.

We do not use any analytics provider, ad network, or data broker. We do not sell, rent, or otherwise share your data with anyone, for any reason, ever.

Your data, your choices

  • Linking a Discord/Minecraft account is entirely optional — the project works anonymously (Tier C) with no linked identity at all, just at a higher corroboration threshold.
  • To unlink or have a linked identity suspended, contact the project maintainer (see below). Since a linked identity is just a Discord ID, unlinking removes essentially all of what we held about you.
  • Since the wire protocol never carries an absolute coordinate, there is no "delete my location history" request to make — that history doesn't exist to begin with.

Legal requests

We don't sell or share data, and we push back on any request — legal or otherwise — that asks for more than what's actually described on this page. In practice, the strongest protection here isn't a promise to resist disclosure; it's that the system is built so there is very little to disclose in the first place, and the one thing that would actually matter (your real location) is architecturally never collected at all.

Changes to this policy

If this changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page. Given the project's whole design premise is collecting less, not more, we don't expect this policy to grow.
